🛡️ Lab Safety Guidelines
Mandatory safety rules · Emergency procedures
⚠️ Mandatory Reading: All students and staff must read and follow these guidelines before entering the chemistry laboratory. Non-compliance may result in injury or disciplinary action.
✅ DO'S — Safe Practices
✓
Always wear PPE: Safety goggles, lab coat, and gloves must be worn at all times when handling chemicals.
✓
Label everything: All containers must be clearly labeled with chemical name, concentration, and date.
✓
Know exit routes: Familiarize yourself with all emergency exits and fire extinguisher locations before beginning work.
✓
Wash hands thoroughly before and after all lab work, even if gloves were worn throughout.
✓
Report all accidents immediately to the lab incharge, no matter how minor they may seem.
✓
Work in small groups of 2-4 students and always have a partner present during experiments.
✓
Dispose chemicals properly by following the waste disposal chart posted near every sink.
✓
Keep the workspace clean: Wipe surfaces before and after use. Return all equipment to designated storage.
❌ DON'TS — Prohibited Actions
✗
Never eat or drink in the laboratory — even water. This is a strict, non-negotiable rule.
✗
Do not smell chemicals directly. Use the wafting technique to detect odors from a safe distance.
✗
Never pipette by mouth. Always use a pipette pump or bulb. Mouth pipetting is strictly prohibited.
✗
Do not mix unknown chemicals. Only mix chemicals as instructed in the experiment protocol.
✗
No mobile phones during active experiments. Distraction is a leading cause of lab accidents.
✗
Never run or rush in the laboratory. Sudden movements near chemicals or glassware are dangerous.
✗
Do not touch eyes after handling chemicals, even with gloves. Always wash hands first.
✗
Never work alone in the laboratory. At least two people must be present at all times.
🚨 EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
🔥
Chemical Fire: Use CO₂ extinguisher. Never use water on chemical fires. Evacuate immediately and call 101.
🧴
Chemical Spill on Skin: Flush with large amounts of water for 15 minutes. Remove contaminated clothing. Seek medical attention.
👁️
Eye Exposure: Use emergency eye wash station immediately. Flush eyes for 15 minutes. Do NOT rub eyes.
🌬️
Gas Leak / Fumes: Evacuate immediately. Turn off all gas valves. Open windows. Do not use electrical switches.
🩺
Injury / Cuts from Glass: Do not remove glass. Apply pressure. Inform lab incharge immediately for first aid.
📞
Emergency Contacts: Lab Incharge: +91 98765 43210 · Ambulance: 108 · Fire: 101 · Poison Control: 1800-116-117
